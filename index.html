Hi There!

My name is Hamza Ruzayqat and I am currently an Associate Editor at Statistics & Computing (Nov 2022 - Present) and a Reasearch Scientist at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) in the group of Computational Probability (COMPPROB) led by Prof. Ajay Jasra. I was a Post-Doctoral Researcher at KASUT from Nov 2019 - Sep 2022. In May 2019, I obtained my PhD and Concurrent Masters degrees in Mathematics from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, TN, USA. In 2012, I earned my Bachelor degree in Physics and Mathematics from Birzeit University, Birzeit, Palestine.

Research

My research focuses on the design, development, and use of efficient and theoretically rigorous stochastic algorithms for data analysis and modeling of real-world problems in science, engineering, and economics. Such problems include, for example, numerical weather forecasting and stock market index prediction. Among the areas in which I work are:

  • Learn more

Monte Carlo methods

This includes Markov chain Monte Carlo methods, sequential Monte Carlo methods and debiasing schemes.

  • Learn more

Data assimilation

This includes particle filtering, ensemble Kalman/Kalman-Bucy filtering and lagged filtering. (These are still related to Monte Carlo)

  • Learn more

Uncertainty Quantification

This includes online and offline parameter calibration (estimation) and inverse problems.

  • Learn more

Bayesian statistics

This includes Bayesian theory, Bayesian inference and unbiased Bayesian inference.
